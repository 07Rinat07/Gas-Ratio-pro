# Engineering Research and External Source Policy

Status: mandatory development policy

External research is permitted and encouraged when it is legal, attributable and technically reviewable.

## Permitted sources

- official standards and vendor-neutral specifications;
- official product/SDK documentation;
- peer-reviewed papers and technical publications;
- open-source repositories with a clear license;
- public test datasets with explicit reuse terms;
- government and academic geological data portals;
- official examples and reference implementations.

## Required workflow

1. Define the engineering question and acceptance criteria.
2. Locate the authoritative specification.
3. Compare mature implementations and licenses.
4. Record the decision in the component registry or architecture decision.
5. Implement behind a project-owned interface.
6. Add conformance, security and regression tests.
7. Update Russian, Kazakh and English user/developer documentation.

Search results, blog posts and generated answers may help discovery, but they are not authoritative evidence by themselves. Critical formulas, file-layout rules and regulatory behavior must be checked against primary sources.
